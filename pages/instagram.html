Instagram

Social Media • Privacy Rating: 11/10

Privacy Rating 11/10

As a core part of Meta's social media empire, Instagram shares many of the same invasive data collection practices as its parent company, Facebook.

Pros & Cons

✅
Data Controls: You can limit (but not fully restrict) how your information is used by third-parties and Instagram.
✅
Data Portability: You can request access to and deletion of your account data.
✅
Policy Change Notifications: Users are typically notified one week before any terms of service are changed.
✅
Content Ownership: You maintain ownership of the content you post.
✅
Audience Control: You can choose with whom you share content (e.g., public vs. private accounts).
❌
Shadow Profiles: Data about you is stored even if you don't have an Instagram account, for example, through a friend's contact list sync.
❌
Heavy Data Collection: Employs extremely heavy amounts of data collection, user tracking, and device fingerprinting.
❌
Platform Controversies: Has faced controversies involving camera access without permission, poor content moderation, and the proliferation of harmful content.
❌
Identity in Ads: Your identity and profile information can be used in advertisements shown to other users, potentially revealing personal lifestyle details.
❌
No E2E Encryption: Because nothing is end-to-end encrypted by default, Instagram can see all messages and comments. Deleted content may still be stored on backup servers.
❌
Third-Party Data Sharing: Your personal information is shared with a wide network of third-party advertisers and partners.
❌
Username Control: Instagram reserves the right to change or remove usernames at any time.
